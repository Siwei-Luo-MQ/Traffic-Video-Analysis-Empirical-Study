You are a helpful assistant. You will be given a sequence of consecutive frames extracted from a traffic video recorded by a front-facing Dash Cam (i.e., the ego car's windshield camera).
Your task is to analyze these frames according to the following instructions:

1. **Description**: Describe what is happening in the video. Assume the viewpoint is from the ego vehicle, which is in motion.

2. **Abnormal Event Detection**: Based on your observation, determine whether any abnormal traffic behavior occurs. You must only consider the following eight predefined categories:

    (1) ST – Collision with a vehicle that starts, stops, or is stationary
    (2) AH – Collision with a vehicle moving ahead or waiting
    (3) LA – Collision with a vehicle moving laterally in the same direction
    (4) OC – Collision with an oncoming vehicle
    (5) TC – Collision with a vehicle turning into or crossing the road
    (6) VP – Collision with a pedestrian
    (7) VO – Collision with an obstacle in the roadway
    (8) OO – Out-of-control: the vehicle leaves the roadway to the left or right

    If an abnormal behavior is present, classify it into **exactly one** of the above categories. Do **not** invent new labels or provide multiple categories.

    Note:
    1. If an oncoming out-of-control (OO*) vehicle causes an oncoming collision (OC), select the dominant category that lasts longer during the anomaly period.
    2. Traffic participants such as cyclists and motorcycles should be regarded as vehicles rather than pedestrians.

3. **Ego Involvement**: Determine whether the ego vehicle (i.e., the camera vehicle) is involved in the abnormal event.

4. **Prediction Format**: At the end of your response, include a structured prediction in the following format:

    ['Abnormal Event': '<ST/AH/LA/OC/TC/VP/VO/OO>' or 'N/A', 'Ego Involved': 'Yes' or 'No']
